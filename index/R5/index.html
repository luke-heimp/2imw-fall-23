R5: My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge

1. I agree with Laurel Schwulst’s assertion that individual websites are more effective than social media. Social media websites exist within the attention economy. They do not exist for self-expression and connection, but rather as a means for advisors to show people ads. The concept of expressing yourself within social media’s confines only exists to keep users trapped on web pages so large companies can show advertisements. In contrast, individual websites exist as a unique means of self-expression, especially for artists. People can use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to make anything imaginable to share their personalities with the world. This freedom is infinite compared to the increasingly standardized world of social media boxes. Individual websites can build unique and engaging interactions and user experiences. Individuals with their own websites can create work that exists on its own. This isolation gives their content more importance as opposed to it being one of a billion pages fighting for the user’s attention. Individual websites can set the record straight with more freedom of expression and creative possibilities to express oneself. With individual websites creators can own 100% of what they host without relying on middlemen. People aren't boxes so we should not box them in, that’s why design flexibility and freedom are so important. One consideration not mentioned in the article is that personal websites are great, but they exist in an isolated ecosystem, making them far more difficult for people to find as opposed to the large deliberately interconnected webs of social media platforms.

2. Website as snowman A snowman is a human creation existing as a form of creative expression. Snowmen are made out of common materials available to many people, but only a certain few go through the effort of getting bundled up and making them. Snowmen exist as representations of people despite obviously not having life themselves. Based on a snowman’s appearance people ascribe it a personality and meaning. A snowman is never complete, it can always be changed. The appearance of a snowman is in flux, it can be altered, therefore changing its personality. Some people build snowmen for the joy of creation, while others build snowmen as public exhibitions. In spite of their novelty, snowmen can be used as a tool for communication. The conditions to build a snowman must be right, they cannot be rushed. When the weather changes snowmen disappear. Without the favourable hosting of wet winter snow, the ephemeral creation fades away never to be seen again.
