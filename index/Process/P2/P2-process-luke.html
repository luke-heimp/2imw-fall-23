Luke Heimpel P2: Poster Process

Original Layout

Upon choosing the What Happens After the Environment content package, I researched the event and came up with a plan for how I wanted to visually communicate the information on a landing page. I decided that this very professional and serious event needed a page that conveyed the importance of the topic while also not being completely alienating. In my original layout, I tried to balance readability and the importance of the content, while still being engaging and hopeful. I designed my layout in Adobe Illustrator.

I also looked into writer and activist Naomi Klein, and Canadian Centre for Architecture Director Mirko Zardini.

Based off of this layout I created an HTML and CSS page using flex boxes to compartmentalize the different sections of the page

Final Poster Page

After some in-class feedback from Prof. Ha, I decided to change some of my typefaces and colours to better suit the tone of the event. I also added some more sections to the website to make the information more clear.

This project taught me a lot. I learned more about flexboxes, typography different types of webpage, and motivated design.
