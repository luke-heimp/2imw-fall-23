Luke Heimpel Index Process

Ideation

The process of creating my index was quite difficult. Because we were learning how to use HTML, CSS, and Java while we were expected to work on your index, my skill set was constantly changing. The hardest part was finding a thematic idea to explore through my index. I kept looking at Peter’s examples, especially the crane game index. I wanted to come up with something that took a familiar web-based situation and twisted it. Eventually, I thought of exploring the idea of e-commerce. Buying products from websites has become so ubiquitous that we take it for granted. Amazon has conditioned its customers to expect incredibly rapid delivery times. The convenience is almost too much. I wanted to use the idea of an e-commerce website to deliver my index while introducing some inconvenience so the user has to work for their final delivery of my index materials. I looked at the design of popular e-commerce sites and identified the design elements that I wished to replicate. I also wanted to incorporate lots of illustrations in this project. I reflected on many of the flash games I would play as a child and their implementation of intractable illustrations. There was one set of games based on the adventure/mystery style where the user would interact with an illustrated environment by clicking on the separate elements. I hoped to replicate this explorative and playful idea as part of my index.
